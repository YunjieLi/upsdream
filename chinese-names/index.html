Read Chinese Names

Understand Pinyin, the standard phonetic system for Mandarin Chinese: rules and exceptions

General Rules

  • It always breaks into syllables of consonant + vowel
    • Vowel only is OK
  • There's no silent letters*
  • In names, the tones are usually dropped

Vowels

a, o, e, i, u, ü
ai, ei, ui, ao, ou, iu, ie, üe
an, en, in, un
ang, eng, ing, ong
er
Special rules
  • Xüe → Xue, this applies to j,q,x
  • Lü → Lv? Lu? Lyu?

Consonants

b, p, m, f, d, t, n, l, g (always gh), k, h
z, c, s, r
zh, ch, sh
j, q, x
y, w
Special rules
  • i as a placeholder vowel: zhi, chi, shi, ri, zi, ci, si
  • Can't start with i, u, ü: yi, wu, yu, ye, yue, yuan, yin, yun, ying

Random examples

yunjie li, ran wei, menghan, yang yang, xiaoming zhang
